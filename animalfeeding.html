Animal Feeding Practices and Dietary Needs Precision Feeding: Ongoing research is refining feeding strategies to optimize nutrient delivery and cater to the specific needs of animals at different stages of growth. Use of Agro-Industrial By-Products: There's an increasing use of agro-industrial by-products and commercial concentrate mixes in animal feed. This helps in coping with feed scarcity and improving sustainability. Safety and Quality: The Codex Alimentarius Code of Practice on Good Animal Feeding emphasizes the importance of feed safety and quality. This includes preventing contamination and ensuring the nutritional adequacy of feed. Wildlife Conservation Efforts Protected Areas: India, for example, has established over 850 protected areas, including national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, to safeguard its rich biodiversity. Project Tiger: This initiative focuses on preserving the natural habitats of Bengal tigers and reducing human encroachment. It involves monitoring tiger populations and implementing strategies to maintain a healthy breeding population. International Agreements: Global efforts include agreements like the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES) and the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD), which aim to protect endangered species and their habitats.
